Offshore Wind Powers Illegal Bitcoin Mining Operation

Story Details below:

Bitcoin & Wind Power, are they real?

Authorities discovered over three thousand electronic rigs used illegally by Bitcoin miners in an underwater dome not far from the offshore windmills that powered them last week. Recreational scuba divers from the resort city of Virginia Beach discovered the rigs which were using stolen renewable electricity to power the operation. The utility company that owns the offshore windfarm was unaware of the drain on power generation since the total output was still exceeding original estimates.

The devices were seized between April and June and have an estimated value of $5.3 million USD. Eight people associated with the Black Manta crime syndicate were reportedly arrested for being involved in mining activities using stolen electricity. Officials from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration were impressed with the structural complexity and design of the protective underwater dome which appeared to be built in sections and assembled on site by what must have been a team of highly skilled divers.

Aquaman to assit with illegal device disposal

The confiscated devices will be disposed of by submerging them in the Puerto Rican trench where the water pressure at depths of more than 8,400 meters will crush their components while the salt water corrodes their circuits.

As the cryptocurrency market surged earlier this year, it brought into focus the massive energy consumption in mining digital coins. The energy-intensive “proof-of-work” process, through which Bitcoins are earned/generated, makes computers compete to solve complex mathematical equations. Given the competitive element in the quest for valuable cryptocurrency, powerful mining rigs — PCs purpose-built to maximize mining rewards — are preferred by cryptocurrency miners.

Electricity theft is a major issue in regions where Bitcoin is mined as some miners use illegal means to secure the power supply necessary to make a big profit from cryptocurrency mining. Because of the additional pressure it puts on the electric grid, authorities have to often resort to load sharing that results in regular outages.
